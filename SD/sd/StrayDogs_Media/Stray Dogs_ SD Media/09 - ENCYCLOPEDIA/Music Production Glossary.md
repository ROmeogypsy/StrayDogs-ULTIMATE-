# MUSIC PRODUCTION GLOSSARY

## EQUIPMENT AT THE KENNEL

**Neve 8078 Console**
- Vintage analog mixing board (32-channel)
- Worth £50,000+ (Dame's prized possession)
- Warm, fat sound (why SD Media tracks sound different from digital production)
- Requires maintenance (Wags learns this from Dame)

**Neumann U87**
- Industry-standard condenser microphone
- What Angel records with (as Dog-Prophet, SF Beats, Dog-Law)
- Captures detail, breath, room tone

**SM7B**
- Backup mic, dynamic (less sensitive)
- What K-9 prefers (grime aggression, less room noise)

**DI Boxes**
- Direct input for instruments
- Limited use (SD Media is mostly vocal-based)

## TECHNIQUES USED

**Syllabic Embedding** — [[Syllabic Embedding]]
Multiple semantic layers in single phonetic sequences. Angel's signature.

**Backwards Text** — [[Backwards Text]]
Zed's cipher communication. Reversed phrases in tracks.

**Fibonacci Flow** — K-9's structural technique
Syllable count follows Fibonacci sequence: 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21

**Chopper Flow** — K-9
Fast-paced, Tech N9ne influence, Midwest chop style

**Controlled Stutter** — Angel (Track 03)
"Th-tho" — on-beat mockery of K-9's nervous stumble

## PRODUCTION TERMS

**Mixing**
- Balancing levels, EQ, compression
- Dame does this for all SD Media tracks
- Minimal mixing = authentic sound (SD Media philosophy)

**Mastering**
- Final polish, volume consistency
- Dame also does this (rare for one person to do both)

**Room Tone**
- Ambient sound of recording space
- Deliberately kept in SD Media tracks (you can hear The Kennel)

**One-Take**
- No edits, recorded start-to-finish
- Track 09 was one-take phone recording

---

## DAME'S PHILOSOPHY

"If you can't do it in one take, you're not ready. The mistakes are proof it's real."

- No autotune
- No pitch correction
- Breaks/skits included (Track 03: "Think you got it bruv?")
- Room tone preserved (bass hum, breath, movement)
