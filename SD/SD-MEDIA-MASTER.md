## TECHNIQUES

### SYLLABIC EMBEDDING
*Primary user: Angel Reyes. Also: K-9.*

Multiple semantic layers within single phonetic sequences. The listener hears one thing. A second reading exists. Sometimes a third.

| Line | Surface | Embedded |
|------|---------|----------|
| "Wine dykes → Whines rights" | Wine enthusiasts | LGBTQ+ rights (K-9's bisexuality) |
| "H is in ome ijo" | Letter H in "home" | Spanish: *hijo* (son) — H is in homie AND hijo |
| "bucks back the dear is gone" | Money/deer fled | Money gone / dear person gone / Dmitri gone |
| "Do ne rro fo flo" | Sounds | Reversed: "Dinero for flow" — *dinero* = money |

## CANON RULES

1. **Prose only.** No bullet points in scenes. No system readouts. Write like a novelist, not a database.

2. **Stray's speech:** He has NOW spoken a full paragraph aloud — once. He CAN speak. He CHOOSES when. Previously 1–3 words only. Track every word. Do NOT normalise his speech.

3. **Show, don't tell.** Never write "he was angry." Write what anger looks like in that specific character's body. 2–3 distinctive visual details per character per scene.

4. **K-9's #Dominance trait:** Internal monologue ONLY. No character reacts to it. No narrator announces it. It leaks through subtext and body language — never stated.

5. **This document is canon.** If it's not here, it hasn't happened. Never invent lore. Stop and ask if unsure.

6. **Consequences are permanent.** If a character is hurt, they stay hurt. If a secret slips, it's slipped. No retcons.

7. **Every scene has stakes.** Before writing: What could go wrong? What does each character want? What are they afraid of?

8. **Dialect is earned.** Wrong slang breaks immersion faster than no slang.

9. **The community matters.** North Ends isn't a backdrop — it's a character. The Dogs are loved because they protect.

10. **Angel's lyrics are sacred.** User writes them. AI describes the room listening, the impact landing, the bodies in space responding. AI never invents Angel's bars.
