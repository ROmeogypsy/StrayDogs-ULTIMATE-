## THE METHODOLOGY: SHORTEST VECTOR LOGIC

"Shortest vector" here means: **which Tech N9ne project is the closest stylistic/thematic/technical match** to each Stray Dogs entry — measured by the fewest "degrees of separation" across: flow style, theme, lyrical density, subject matter, and structural device.

Tech N9ne's discography key alignment points used:
- **Chopper/rapid-fire** → *Everready (The Religion)*, *Absolute Power*, *Worldwide Choppers*
- **Dark horror/psychological** → *K.O.D. (King of Darkness)*, *Anghellic*, *Tormented*
- **Spiritual/sermon/street ministry** → *The Sermon (Sickology 101 era)*, *Special Effects*, *The Storm*
- **UK grime/dense syllabic** → *Strangeulation* series (Cypher energy)
- **Grief, loss, dedication** → *Enterfear*, *Planet*, *N9na*
- **Comedy/flex/crew dynamics** → *Collabos* series, *Caribou Lou* era
- **Bay Area/Hyphy fusion** → *Dominion* era crossover cuts
- **Independent identity** → *The Calm Before the Storm*, *Killer*

**TIN EYED TIFF (Stray):** Stutter-as-device (phonemic breaks written in), phase structure, ballistics-as-narrative. Density ~2.0.

**MARKS AUNT LETTERS:** 7-movement suite — the most ambitious structural piece. H-alliteration sermon, entrepreneurial outro, global-grind montage. Density ~2.2 across movements.

**PROPER WICKED INNIT (Chew-Toy, Zed, Rax):** Comedy as disarmament. Zed's verse uses "remedial/variable" as mathematical diss. "Bespoke by dope hits" — luxury as flow metaphor.

**BACK THE KNIFE (K-9, R-Dogg-ind):** Countdown-to-violence mapped to moral choice. "Back that knife back to its back" — putting weapon away = growth. Density 1.9.

**STRAY DOGS ANTHEM (K-9, Chew-Toy, Zed):** Pack formation anthem. "Every dog has its day" as promise, not cliché. K-9's verse has the most sustained alliterative run in the anthology outside Chalk & Crown.

**THE DIALOGUE / NEWSBREAK:** Skits function as structural devices — world-building that elevates lyric tracks by contrast.
